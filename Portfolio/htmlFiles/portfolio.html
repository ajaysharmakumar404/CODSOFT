  2. REDUCING TRAFFIC CONGESTION THROUGH REINFORCEMENT LEARNING TECHNIQUES IN SUMO under Mr. SATISH KUMAR NEGI

    • Description: By evaluating different parameters in a SUMO (Simulation of Urban MObility) generated dataset, our algorithm strives to attain optimal outcomes. Initially, we conducted a baseline simulation, followed by the implementation of Q-Learning, Deep Q-Learning, SARSA, and Bias Q-Learning algorithms. Subsequently, we compared the results derived from these algorithms. This research endeavor aims to enhance efficiency in bustling urban areas by significantly alleviating traffic congestion.

    • Technologies Used: Simulation of Urban Mobility (SUMO) , Traffic Control Interfae (TraCI), Unity, PyTorch, Python, Visual Studio Code (VS Code)

    • Outcome: Overall, the proposed model addresses the critical issue of urban traffic congestion. While it has made significant strides by implementing various reinforcement learning algorithms, the potential for improvement remains vast. Quantum computing emerges as a promising avenue to enhance accuracy and efficiency, potentially revolutionizing the training process. However, the project faces challenges such as access to real data, funding constraints, and environmental inconsistencies. Recommendations for future implementation include leveraging advancements in electronic and quantum computing, exploring hierarchical methods for optimization, incorporating simulations of road accidents, and integrating real-world data for more accurate analyses. Ultimately, the goal is to deploy the model in real traffic systems, supported by robust sensor capabilities, to alleviate urban congestion effectively.
